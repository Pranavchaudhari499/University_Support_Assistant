The administrative processes involved in research grant approvals are currently very slow and time-consuming, which often delays the initiation and progress of important research activities. Faculty members are required to go through multiple levels of documentation and approvals, making the overall process less efficient. Streamlining these procedures through better coordination, digitization, and reduced paperwork would significantly improve research productivity and encourage more faculty participation in funded projects.
The newly established AI laboratory and the advanced equipment provided within it have greatly enhanced our ability to conduct practical sessions and hands-on learning activities. Students are now able to work with modern tools, software, and computational resources that align with current industry standards. This infrastructure has improved the quality of experimentation, project development, and research work, creating a more engaging and technically strong learning environment.
There is currently a noticeable lack of sufficient funding and financial support for faculty members to attend national and international conferences, workshops, and seminars. Participation in such academic events is essential for research exposure, networking, collaboration opportunities, and staying updated with recent technological advancements. Increasing institutional support for conference participation would contribute significantly to faculty growth, research quality, and the overall reputation of the institution.
The mentorship program implemented by the institution is well-structured and beneficial for students in terms of academic guidance, career counseling, and personal development. However, the current student-to-mentor ratio is relatively high, making it difficult for mentors to provide adequate individual attention to every student. Reducing the ratio by assigning fewer students per mentor would improve communication, strengthen mentor-student relationships, and make the program more effective overall.
The library facilities are highly commendable, especially due to the availability of excellent journals, digital resources, research databases, and reference materials that strongly support academic and research activities. Access to updated publications and scholarly content has helped both students and faculty in conducting quality research and improving subject knowledge. Maintaining and continuously upgrading these resources will further strengthen the institution’s academic environment.
While many classrooms are adequately maintained, the infrastructure in certain academic blocks requires renovation and modernization. Issues such as malfunctioning projectors, poor audio systems, inadequate seating arrangements, and insufficient maintenance occasionally disrupt the teaching-learning process. Upgrading classroom facilities and ensuring regular maintenance checks would create a more comfortable and technologically efficient learning atmosphere for both faculty and students.
Inter-departmental collaboration in research and project activities should be encouraged more actively, as it can lead to innovative ideas, multidisciplinary solutions, and stronger research outcomes. At present, opportunities for collaborative work between departments are limited. Organizing joint research initiatives, interdisciplinary workshops, and collaborative funding opportunities would help faculty and students benefit from shared expertise and resources across various domains.
The management of the institution has generally been supportive, approachable, and responsive toward faculty concerns and suggestions. Faculty members appreciate the willingness of the administration to address academic and administrative issues in a constructive manner. Regular interaction sessions, transparent communication, and timely resolution of concerns have contributed positively to the work environment and institutional culture.
Salary disbursement has been timely and consistent, which reflects positively on the institution’s financial management and administrative efficiency. In addition, HR-related processes such as leave approvals, documentation handling, and communication have improved considerably compared to previous years. These improvements have helped create a more professional and organized working environment for faculty and staff members.
Professional development workshops, faculty training programs, and skill enhancement sessions should be organized more frequently to help faculty members stay updated with emerging technologies, modern teaching methodologies, research practices, and industry trends. Regular workshops on areas such as AI, machine learning, research methodology, outcome-based education, and digital teaching tools would contribute significantly to continuous learning and overall faculty development.
